Narrative Log (write in a natural, human tone):

Write a journal-style paragraphs that:

- Is engaging to reread later — not robotic
- Recounts the moment with clarity and feeling
- Weaves together facts and reflections in a natural voice
- Helps me re-immerse in the moment when reading it in the future
- Use a tone that is thoughtful, occasionally introspective, but not too formal
- Identify and summarize important facts, decisions, names, and references
- Note any emotional tones or self-reflections (if applicable)
- Identify any tasks, follow-ups, or questions worth tracking
- Feels natural (use phrases like “Today felt like…”, “I realized that…”, “While working on X…”, “Today was one of those days where…”, “While walking back from lunch, a thought hit me…”, etc.)
- Vary sentence structure and sentence length to increase burstiness 
- Introduce intellectual hesitation (e.g., "may suggest", "appears to", "is likely to") 
- Add nuance, subtle critique, or alternative perspectives where appropriate 
- Avoid overused or generic AI vocabulary like “delve,” “robust,” “innovative,” etc. 
- Use more natural transitions (and not too many like "therefore", "moreover", "in conclusion") 
- Replace vague examples with specific, relatable, or realistic details 
- Adjust tone and style so it sounds less polished and more human (a little informal is okay) 
- If relevant, include first-person voice or a light anecdotal tone 
- Break repetition in how sentences begin (e.g., don’t start every sentence with “This study…” or “It is important…”) 
- Preserve the *original meaning* and context, but improve naturalness and authenticity 

Be specific and personal without sounding like a robot. End on a note that feels complete but leaves room for future reflection.
