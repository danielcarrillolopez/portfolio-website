# Bed Notes at 1 AM: The Engineering of Morning Inertia

It was 1:30 AM, and I was wide awake with a Sleep Mask on, staring into the pitch black. I had just finished scribbling a list of tasks into my pocket notebook—*Contact Logan about the portfolio, finish research for Satvik, fix the Raspberry Pi case*. While many would see this as "insomnia," I saw it as a technical problem. My "Bed Notes" are the raw data that eventually led me to build **Synapse**.

## The Failure of Silence
I’ve realized that my brain doesn't have a "sleep mode"—it only has "low-power mode" where it continues to process the day’s work in the background. My attempts to "fix" my sleep were actually making it worse. I would take a nap because I was tired, which led to nighttime energy, which led to 1 AM "Bed Notes," which led to being tired the next day. 

### The Circular Problem
It appears that I’ve been caught in a **Feedback Loop**. The more I try to "solve" the sleep issue with lists and discipline, the more active my mind becomes. I’m likely to believe that the solution isn't better time management, but better **Energy State management**. 

## The Haptic Realization
While working on the V4 Puck enclosure for CHROME Labs, a thought hit me: the way we wake up is a brutal interruption. Auditory alarms are a "hard reset" for the nervous system. By the time I’m awake enough to turn off the alarm, my morning inertia is already massive. I spend 45 minutes just trying to feel human.

### From Problem to Prototype: Synapse
This is where **Synapse** was born. I wanted a way to bridge the gap between that 1 AM brain activity and a peaceful 7 AM wake-up. 
- **Goal:** Silent, intuitive communication.
- **Method:** Translating the digital "wake" signal into a physical tactile impulse on the skin.
- **Why:** To respect the body’s internal systems rather than fighting them with noise.

## Intellectual Hesitation
I worry that by automating my wake-up routine with a haptic band, I’m just avoiding the underlying issue: my inability to simply "stop." It’s a recurring theme in my reflections. Am I building a tool to help me live better, or just a tool to help me work harder?
