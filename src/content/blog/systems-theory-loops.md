# Loops, Not Lines: An Engineer's Guide to Personal Systems

Today I sat down with a motor driver that wouldn't stop oscillating, and it hit me: my own life is doing the exact same thing. I’ve been reading about **General Systems Theory**, and it’s shifted how I view my "problems." In engineering, we’re taught linear causality—*A causes B, so fix A*. But in the human system, everything is a loop.

## The Cake and the System
While reviewing my notes on systems, I kept coming back to the "cake" metaphor. You can't understand a cake by just looking at an egg or a bag of flour. The whole is greater than the sum of its parts. I realized that I’ve been trying to "fix" my sleep by looking at the "eggs" (coffee, naps, lists) instead of looking at the **cake** ( the entire self-maintaining cycle of my day).

### Circular Causality: The Sleep Trap
My sleep isn't a "problem to be solved"—it’s a loop maintaining itself. It may suggest that my "solutions" are actually the fuel. 
- I’m tired during the day (Input).
- I take a "strategic" nap (Process).
- I have a surge of energy at 11 PM (Output).
- I work because I feel like I'm falling behind (Feedback).
- I can't sleep at 1 AM (Loop Closed).

It appears likely that by trying to "intervene" at the end of the loop with coffee or list-making, I’m just reinforcing the stability of the system. In systems theory, this is **Negative Feedback**—not "bad" feedback, but feedback that resists change and maintains the status quo (Homeostasis).

## Morphostasis vs. Morphogenesis
I’ve realized that my current system is too weighted toward *morphostasis*—stability-seeking. I say I want to change, but my internal beliefs push back to restore the old balance. My therapist pointed out that even dysfunctional patterns feel "safe" because they are familiar. To get to *morphogenesis*—actual growth—I have to be willing to let the system become chaotic for a moment.

### Intellectual Hesitation
I worry that by viewing everything as a "system," I’m stripping away the human element. Is my family just a collection of feedback loops? It feels a bit cold. But then again, if I can't see the pattern, I'm just a victim of it. 

## Self-Reflection
I am not just *in* the system; I *am* the system. My judgment of people who aren't "productive" is a feedback loop that isolates me, which makes me retreat further into work, which reinforces the belief that work is my only value. It’s a tight, efficient, and exhausting circle.

## Tasks & Questions

- [ ] Identify one "Transactional" behavior I can skip this week to test the system's resistance.
- [ ] Research how "Positive Feedback Loops" (amplifying loops) can be used to accelerate technical learning without causing burnout.

In the end, you can't think your way out of a loop. You have to act your way out. You have to be the component that refuses to follow the signal.
