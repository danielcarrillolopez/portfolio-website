Since childhood, I knew that I wanted to dedicate myself to engineering. That’s why I took up every single opportunity that I was able to participate in to accelerate my skills and career readiness. This isn't just a list of schools or grades; it's a map of how I learned to create.

## The Self-Driven Foundation
Most of the technical skills I have come from what I taught myself. Learning CAD tools such as **SolidWorks**, **Fusion 360**, and **TinkerCAD**, along with programming languages like **Python**, **Java**, and **C++**, was challenging. But it was worth it because it taught me the most valuable skill of all: the ability to create. 

I wasn’t just learning for the sake of it, but because I felt a need to know. It would often come up: "I wonder how this works." Those self-driven projects led me to understand what it means to have an engineering mindset.

## Key Milestones

### 2022: Entering the FIRST Arena
Joining **Force Green Robotics (10265)** was my entry into competitive engineering. It taught me that technical skill is only half the battle—the other half is collaboration and leadership. Eventually becoming the Technical Captain and Head Designer allowed me to transition from "building things" to "designing systems."

### Summer 2025: The CHROME Lab Internship
My time at **Saint Louis University’s CHROME Lab** was a turning point. Working on the **Adaptive Blind Hockey Puck** project introduced me to the world of academic research. Thanks to my mentors, I was able to learn the ins and outs of user-centered design, prototyping modular shell structures, and working in an interdisciplinary environment. 

### 2025-2026: Senior Year & Beyond
Now, as a senior at **Lafayette High School**, I am focusing on advanced coursework in Physics and Calculus while continuing to lead our robotics team and develop projects like **Synapse**. 

## The Power of Empowering Others
Beyond my own projects, I also wanted to learn about the range of possibilities available to me. I frequently reached out to connect with professionals about their own journeys. This led to my biggest takeaway: **impact grows exponentially when you empower others.**

By teaching someone to design, code, and build, we’ve given them tools to solve any problem they encounter. That’s why I have focused my energy on education. There is only one way to help change the world, and that is by empowering those around us—trailblazing a path for them to follow.

## Final Reflection
Looking back at this timeline, I realize that the "freedom" I was looking for in my early reflections wasn't an exit from the system, but an entry into my own agency. I am no longer just a student; I am a builder who knows that every milestone is just a new starting line.
