## Overview
**Synapse: Haptic Band** is a wearable notification system that replaces auditory alerts with precise tactile feedback. Originally conceived to solve the problem of "alarm fatigue" for heavy sleepers, the project evolved into a safety tool for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community, translating critical sounds—like smoke alarms and weather warnings—into unmistakable physical sensations.

## The Problem
Research indicates that **80% of people** struggle to wake up consistently with traditional sound-based alarms. For the **37.5 million Americans** who are deaf or hard-of-hearing, auditory alerts aren't just ineffective; they are inaccessible. Safety tools should not rely on a sense that a user may not possess.

## Technical Implementation
The project has progressed through **five generations of functional prototypes**, focusing on miniaturization and signal clarity.
- **The Brain:** Powered by a **Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32-C3**, providing Wi-Fi and Bluetooth connectivity for smartphone and smart-home integration.
- **Tactile Feedback:** Utilizes precisely calibrated vibration motors to deliver targeted haptic patterns directly to the wrist.
- **Customization:** Users can tailor haptic patterns, ranging from a "gentle pulse" for notifications to a "strong shake" for emergency alerts.

## Entrepreneurial Journey
Synapse is more than a technical project; it is a developing venture. 
- **User Validation:** Conducted interviews and surveys to ensure the haptic intensity and patterns met real-world needs.
- **Pitching:** Presented Synapse to a panel of judges and a live audience at **Maryville University's Battle of the Saints** entrepreneurship competition.
- **Brand Identity:** Developed a full business model canvas, public-facing website, and marketing materials.

## Key Outcomes
- Successfully demonstrated a hardware-software bridge that translates digital alerts into tactile safety.
- Received recognition at Maryville University for the potential social impact of the device.
- Named after the biological bridge between nerve cells, Synapse now functions as a bridge between people and their digital safety ecosystems.
