## Overview
As the Technical Captain and Head Designer for **Force Green Robotics (FTC 10265)**, I lead the design and fabrication of competition-grade robots. Our team operates at the intersection of high-stakes competitive robotics and community STEM outreach. My role involves not only modeling complex assemblies in CAD but also mentoring rookie members and managing our relationship as a **Team REV** brand ambassador.

## The Challenge
FTC robots are constrained by a 18-inch starting cube and an 8-motor limit. To compete at the World Championship level, we had to solve for extreme space efficiency and actuator optimization. 

### Post-Worlds Iteration Goals:
- **Actuator Sharing:** Implementing **Power Take-Off (PTO)** systems to use drivetrain motors for vertical mechanisms.
- **Rigging Reliability:** Transitioning to **Dyneema rope** to minimize "creep" and stretch in high-tension lift systems.
- **Sensor Integration:** Designing 3D-printed mounts for **Limelight cameras** on servo-swivel arms to maximize field visibility for AprilTag localization.

## Technical Implementation
- **PTO Systems:** Researching and prototyping shifter systems that allow a single motor to alternate between driving the chassis and powering a heavy-duty winch.
- **Materials Science:** Utilizing **splicing tape** and specialized rigging techniques to ensure precision in elevator heights.
- **CAD & Prototyping:** Iterating on a modular "bare minimum" structural design, slimming down arm and elbow components to increase speed without sacrificing torque.
- **Odometry:** Integrating **Go Builda odometry pods** with a dedicated co-processor for real-time localization.

## Leadership & Mentorship
Engineering excellence is only 50% of the game. I’ve implemented a "Culture of Expression" within the team:
- **Rookie Onboarding:** Giving every new member a pocket notebook to document their learning journey.
- **The 5th Grade Standard:** Training senior members to explain complex technical concepts in accessible language for both judges and junior teammates.

## Key Outcomes
- **World Championship 1st Place Design:** Awarded top honors for mechanical design and engineering excellence at the FIRST Tech Challenge World Championships.
- **World Championship Qualifier:** Successfully rebuilt our robot "Eileen" under extreme pressure during the competition.
- **Team REV Sponsorship:** Secured and maintained official sponsorship through consistent technical documentation and brand representation.
- **Dean's List Semifinalist:** Recognized for individual leadership and impact within the FIRST community.
