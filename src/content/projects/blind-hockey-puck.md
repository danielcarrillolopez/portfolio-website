## Project Overview
This project, a partnership between **Saint Louis University’s CHROME Lab**, **Lighthouse for the Blind**, and the **St. Louis Blue Blind Hockey Club**, aimed to revolutionize the puck for visually impaired athletes. Traditional metal pucks are silent when stationary and easily damaged. I developed a modular, hybrid-material enclosure that provides consistent sound feedback while surviving high-velocity impacts.

### The Problem
- **Invisibility of Stillness:** Ball-bearing pucks go silent when they stop moving, creating "dead zones" in the game.
- **Economic Inefficiency:** Metal pucks ($60–$80) are costly to replace when they shatter during play, leading to athletes training with compromised gear.

### Technical Solution
- **The Core:** An **ESP32-C3** managing accelerometer-triggered vibration modes (sonar, pulse, and continuous buzzing).
- **The Armor:** A **modular cage** made from **PLA** (strength) and **TPU** (shock absorption). The design uses a sacrificial module approach—if one part breaks, it costs less than $2 to replace.
- **The Interface:** Wi-Fi remote connectivity for coaches to trigger specific "pings" during training drills.
